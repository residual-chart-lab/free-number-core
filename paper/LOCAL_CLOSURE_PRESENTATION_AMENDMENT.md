# Local Closure Presentation Amendment

**Status:** proved replacement for the construction step in `paper/free_numbers.tex`  
**Date:** 2026-07-12  
**Scope:** the local closure \(F_1\) only

## 1. Why this amendment exists

The current paper states the quaternion relations for \(I,J,K\) and then proves that the resulting four-dimensional algebra is isomorphic to \(\mathbb H\). That conclusion is correct, but the construction should make associativity, dimension, and linear independence explicit rather than leaving them implicit in the phrase “local closure.”

This amendment supplies an exact associative presentation.

## 2. Definition by an associative quotient

Let

\[
U=\operatorname{span}_{\mathbb R}\{u,v\}
\]

be a two-dimensional real vector space, and let \(T(U)\) be its unital tensor algebra. Let \(I\) be the two-sided ideal generated by

\[
u^2+1,\qquad v^2+1,\qquad uv+vu.
\]

Define

\[
oxed{
F_1:=T(U)/I.
}
\]

Because \(T(U)\) is associative and \(I\) is a two-sided ideal, \(F_1\) is a unital associative real algebra. Write the images of \(u,v\) in the quotient by the same letters and set

\[
K:=uv.
\]

The defining relations are

\[
u^2=v^2=-1,\qquad uv=-vu.
\]

This is the real Clifford algebra \(\mathrm{Cl}_{0,2}\) in a two-generator presentation.

## 3. Spanning lemma

### Lemma 3.1

Every element of \(F_1\) is a real linear combination of

\[
1,\qquad u,\qquad v,\qquad uv.
\]

### Proof

Every element of \(T(U)\) is a linear combination of words in \(u,v\). Use \(vu=-uv\) to move every \(u\) to the left of every \(v\), and use \(u^2=v^2=-1\) to reduce every exponent modulo two. Thus every word reduces to one of

\[
1,\quad u,\quad v,\quad uv
\]

up to sign. Hence

\[
\dim_{\mathbb R}F_1\le4.
\]

\(\square\)

## 4. Quaternionic realization

Define a unital algebra homomorphism

\[
\phi:T(U)\longrightarrow\mathbb H
\]

by

\[
\phi(u)=i,\qquad \phi(v)=j.
\]

The generators of \(I\) lie in \(\ker\phi\), because

\[
i^2=j^2=-1,\qquad ij+ji=0.
\]

Therefore \(\phi\) descends to a unital algebra homomorphism

\[
ar\phi:F_1\longrightarrow\mathbb H.
\]

Its image contains

\[
1,\qquad i,\qquad j,\qquad ij=k,
\]

so \(ar\phi\) is surjective. Consequently

\[
\dim_{\mathbb R}F_1\ge\dim_{\mathbb R}\mathbb H=4.
\]

Together with Lemma 3.1,

\[
\dim_{\mathbb R}F_1=4.
\]

The surjective linear map \(ar\phi:F_1	o\mathbb H\) is therefore an isomorphism.

### Theorem 4.1 — Local quaternionic closure

\[
oxed{
F_1=T(U)/(u^2+1,\ v^2+1,\ uv+vu)\cong_{\mathbb R	ext{-alg}}\mathbb H.
}
\]

Under this isomorphism,

\[
1\mapsto1,\qquad u\mapsto i,\qquad v\mapsto j,\qquad uv\mapsto k.
\]

## 5. What has and has not been proved

This construction proves the local quaternionic algebra from an explicit associative presentation. It does not claim that the three pure directions are three primitive generators: the primitive generator space remains two-dimensional, while the third pure direction is the product \(uv\), equivalently the bivector direction in \(\Lambda^2U\).

It also does not imply that different local copies of \(F_1\) are globally identical. Global chart identity remains a separate rule in the Residual Chart OS.
