# Adjacent-literature audit — initial map

Status: working document, not a novelty certificate.

The purpose of this audit is to identify the strongest neighboring theories before any priority claim is made. Similar vocabulary does not establish equivalence; equally, unfamiliar vocabulary does not establish novelty. Each source must eventually be compared against the actual data, maps, reconstruction theorem, filtration, and composition law of Core v1.

## A. Noncommutative realization theory

### D. Alpay and D. S. Kalyuzhnyi-Verbovetzkii

**Matrix-J-unitary non-commutative rational formal power series**  
arXiv:math/0407387

Relevant features:

- finite-dimensional realizations of noncommutative rational formal power series;
- minimality and uniqueness up to similarity/unitary similarity;
- structured state-space methods.

Current boundary question:

- Does this framework contain an equivalent of all-gap quaternionic insertion responses with a fixed recursive local inverse?
- Does it define an analogue of first contextual-detection depth for states erased by a many-to-one value map?

No equivalence has yet been found.

### J. Volčič

**Matrix coefficient realization theory of noncommutative rational functions**  
arXiv:1505.07472

Relevant features:

- linear realizations of arbitrary noncommutative rational functions;
- minimal realizations and obstruction modules;
- local-global principles and complexity measures.

Current boundary question:

- Is the Core map \(A_n\) reducible to a standard realization of one noncommutative rational function, or is it better understood as a graded family of contextual evaluation tensors?
- Is the residual-depth filtration identifiable with an established obstruction or reachability/observability filtration?

No direct identification has yet been found.

## B. Weighted automata and Hankel realization

### B. Balle, P. Panangaden, and D. Precup

**A Canonical Form for Weighted Automata and Applications to Approximate Minimization**  
arXiv:1501.06841

Relevant features:

- rational series represented by finite weighted automata;
- Hankel matrices and minimal realization dimension;
- approximation and compression of realizations.

Current boundary question:

- Can exact-response profiles be encoded as a rational series whose Hankel realization reproduces \(\Theta\)-peeling?
- If so, does the depth filtration carry information not represented by ordinary minimal state dimension?

The present Core is not yet stated as a weighted automaton or compression-rate result.

## C. Coding theory and state-space realizations

### H. Gluesing-Luerssen and G. Schneider

**State Space Realizations and Monomial Equivalence for Convolutional Codes**  
arXiv:cs/0603049

### Š. Holub

**State spaces of convolutional codes, codings and encoders**  
arXiv:1712.01914

Relevant features:

- state spaces and minimal encoders for convolutional codes;
- equivalence through state-space structure;
- finite-state descriptions of code behavior.

Current boundary question:

- Can the all-gap response map be interpreted as an encoder or syndrome family?
- Does a coding formulation preserve the central distinction that the quaternionic value map remains deliberately non-injective while the enlarged contextual response is exact?
- What would constitute distance, noise, redundancy, or decoding complexity in this model?

Until these questions are answered, Core v1 must not be advertised as an error-correcting code.

## D. Observability and realization of dynamical systems

**Controllability, Observability, Realizability, and Stability of Dynamic Linear Systems**  
arXiv:0901.3764

### J. E. Gough and G. Zhang

**On Realization Theory of Quantum Linear Systems**  
arXiv:1311.1375

Relevant features:

- observability through rank conditions;
- minimal realizations;
- observable and unobservable subspaces.

Current boundary question:

- Is \(F_d^{(n)}\) an instance of an established observability-index filtration after a suitable system realization?
- Does contextual insertion correspond to controlled input, active measurement, or a different operation?

The language of observability is clearly adjacent. The specific finite tensor-grade, all-gap, quaternionic construction still requires comparison.

## E. Tensor and quantum-state tomography

### E. Cruz, F. Baccari, J. Tura, N. Schuch, and J. I. Cirac

**Preparation and verification of tensor network states**  
arXiv:2105.06866

### A. Lidiak et al.

**Quantum state tomography with tensor train cross approximation**  
arXiv:2207.06397

Relevant features:

- complete families of observables that uniquely determine structured states;
- reconstruction from local measurements;
- reduced measurement complexity under structural assumptions.

Current boundary question:

- Are all-gap insertion responses a form of informationally complete tomography for a noncommutative tensor word?
- What is the minimal probe family?
- How does exact algebraic recovery compare with stable numerical tomography?

Core v1 proves exact finite recovery, not noise robustness or sample efficiency.

## F. Quaternionic representation theory and analysis

### I. Frenkel and M. Libine

**Quaternionic Analysis, Representation Theory and Physics**  
arXiv:0711.2699

**Quaternionic Analysis, Representation Theory and Physics II**  
arXiv:1907.01594

Relevant features:

- representation-theoretic decomposition of quaternionic function spaces;
- quaternionic algebraic operations and higher structures;
- harmonic and regular components.

Current boundary question:

- Which identities behind the local inverse \(\Theta^{-1}\) and the spin-depth sectors are standard consequences of quaternionic representation theory?
- Is the exact-response algebra already recognizable as a known module/algebra construction under another name?

The quaternionic identities themselves should not be marketed as new without a precise comparison.

## Current defensible positioning

The strongest safe claim at this stage is structural rather than historical:

> Core v1 gives an explicit finite-grade architecture in which a deliberately non-injective reversed quaternionic value map coexists with exact recovery from canonical contextual insertion responses. One twelve-dimensional local inverse recursively controls every finite tensor grade; first-detection depth yields a nontrivial filtration; and exact-response coordinates compose as a unital associative graded algebra.

## Priority claim withheld pending audit

The following sentence is intentionally not yet approved for the manuscript:

> This is the first construction of its kind.

A possible final sentence, if the audit remains negative, is:

> We have not located in the adjacent realization, coding, tomography, or quaternionic literature an equivalent construction combining all five features: reversed quaternionic compression, canonical all-gap responses, a grade-independent recursive local inverse, finite first-detection depth, and associative exact-response composition.

Even this must be framed as a documented literature result, not an absolute claim over all mathematical literature.
